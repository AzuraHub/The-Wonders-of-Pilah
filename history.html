HISTORY OF BANDAR PILAH
Kuala Pilah, as part of the Federated Malay States, was under British rule before Malaysia’s independence in 19571. The Federated Malay States, established in 1895, consisted of four protected states in the Malay Peninsula—Selangor, Perak, Negeri Sembilan, and Pahang. Kuala Pilah, being a part of Negeri Sembilan, was thus under this federation. During the early 20th century, Kuala Pilah was known for its tin mining industry. There was even a railway line from Kuala Pilah to Bahau, serving the tin mining industry at Parit Tinggi for about 20 years from 1910.
Chinese Comunity,The town had a sizeable Chinese community that existed since these mining days during the early British rule1. This community engaged in trading and provided essential services such as workshops, hairdressing, and traditional coffee shops called kopitiams.
